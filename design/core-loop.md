# Core Gameplay Loop

The player is always doing one of three things: reading the station, making a decision, or waiting for consequences. These nest into each other across time scales. The game never demands fast reflexes — it demands sustained attention.

---

## The 30-Second Loop — Reading the Room

**Player goal:** Understand what is happening right now.

**Player actions:**
- Scan colonist status icons and movement
- Check a resource gauge that is changing unexpectedly
- Hover over a colonist to read their current state
- Notice a social interaction happening between two colonists

**Colony response:**
- Colonists pursue their current highest-priority goal (work task, rest, eating, socializing)
- Resource levels tick up or down based on active systems and colonist load
- Status changes appear as subtle visual signals — posture shifts, idle behaviors, queue buildups

**Feedback:**
- Legible at a glance: a colonist standing still is meaningful; a queue backing up is meaningful; an empty break room is meaningful
- Nothing demands immediate action; everything rewards attention

**Failure mode:**
- Player stops scanning and misses the early signal of a developing problem
- The problem is always visible in retrospect — the signal was there; it was not read

---

## The 5-Minute Loop — The Decision

**Player goal:** Identify a condition that needs a response and choose how to respond.

**Player actions:**
- Read the context around a noticed signal (inspect a colonist, check shift schedule, review resource flow)
- Make one of the core decision types: infrastructure, policy, personnel, crisis, or inaction
- Set a new policy, queue a construction task, adjust a shift assignment, or consciously choose not to intervene

**Colony response:**
- The world continues while the player decides — colonists do not pause and wait
- After the decision, the colony begins adapting to the new condition over the next several minutes
- The response is never instant: a new policy takes time to propagate; a newly built module takes time to become functional; a colonist reassignment takes effect at the next shift change

**Feedback:**
- The decision is logged (visible to the player later)
- Early feedback is ambiguous: the new condition is in place, but outcomes are not yet readable
- The player must wait to see if the decision was correct

**Failure mode:**
- Player makes a decision without enough information and creates a new problem downstream
- Player delays a decision too long and the situation escalates
- Player intervenes where inaction was the right call and disrupts a situation that was resolving itself

## The 30-Minute Loop — Watching Consequences Unfold

**Player goal:** Understand whether previous decisions are working; identify the next significant problem forming.

**Player actions:**
- Monitor the colony as it responds to earlier decisions
- Re-inspect colonists or systems that were flagged earlier
- Notice new relationships forming, new stressors building, new resource trends
- Make a second or third decision that adjusts or responds to the first

**Colony response:**
- Consequences of earlier decisions become legible: a policy change has visibly shifted colonist behavior; a module has come online and changed resource flow; a personnel decision has affected the people involved
- New tensions have had time to develop: a colonist who was stressed is now approaching a threshold; a resource that was marginal is now trending toward shortage
- The colony has a slightly different character than it did 30 minutes ago

**Feedback:**
- The player can now evaluate whether their earlier decision was correct — and trace exactly why the outcome happened
- New signals are appearing that will require attention in the next cycle

**Failure mode:**
- A consequence the player did not anticipate cascades into a crisis before they noticed the early signal
- The player misreads the colony state and intervenes at the wrong time

## The Multi-Hour Loop — The Story Arc

**Player goal:** Navigate a complete arc — a growing tension, an escalating crisis, and its aftermath.

**Player actions:**
- Track a developing situation across many smaller loops: a pair of colonists whose relationship is deteriorating, a resource system under increasing strain, a colonist approaching a behavioral threshold
- Make a series of decisions that either prevent, mitigate, or absorb the crisis
- Deal with the aftermath: damaged relationships, changed colony morale, infrastructure wear, personnel gaps

**Colony response:**
- The crisis arrives — not as a scripted event, but as the natural culmination of accumulated stress across multiple systems
- The colony's response to the crisis depends on who is available, what the relationships are, what the stress levels are — all of which were shaped by the player's prior decisions
- After the crisis, the colony enters a recovery period with a new equilibrium that is different from the one before

**Feedback:**
- The player understands the full cause chain of the crisis — and can identify which of their decisions contributed to it
- The aftermath creates new conditions that will generate the next arc

**Failure mode:**
- Colony fails to recover from a crisis because its recovery capacity was too depleted
- The player addresses the acute crisis but fails to address the underlying structural condition, which will produce another crisis
- Colony collapse: a complete cascade where the player cannot stop the chain and must accept the outcome

---

## The Long-Term Colony Loop — Culture and Calcification

**Player goal:** Understand what kind of colony this has become, and navigate the consequences of that identity.

**Player actions:**
- Observe patterns that have formed from accumulated decisions: which colonists have become informal leaders, which modules are over-used, what the colony's unwritten norms are
- Respond to situations that earlier colonies never generated — because this colony's history created a specific set of social conditions
- Decide whether to resist or work with the colony's emergent culture

**Colony response:**
- The colony has a character that is distinct from any other playthrough: specific relationships, power structures, informal hierarchies, cultural attitudes toward rest, work, conflict, authority
- New colonists who arrive must navigate this existing culture
- Old problems that were patched but not fixed have compounded; old investments that were made correctly are now load-bearing

**Feedback:**
- The player tells a story about this colony that no walkthrough guide could describe
- The Dinner Table Test passes or fails here: is there a story with characters, cause and effect, and an emotional beat?

**Failure mode:**
- Late-game entropy: accumulated social damage has made the colony unmanageable without a reset that the game does not offer
- Player hits a state they cannot improve — but they can trace exactly how they got there
