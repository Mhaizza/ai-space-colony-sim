## 1. Executive Summary

A colonist is a three-layer agent — stable identity, long-term state, short-term state [colonist-agent-model; locked #1] — who behaves by cycling between *executing* a committed task and *deciding* at condition-triggered decision points [decision-loop §2; ADR-02]. Decisions read exactly two inputs: the colonist's own model and a fixed snapshot of the local world in which other colonists are visible only as the player sees them [decision-loop §1; locked #4, #21]. Pressure comes from five closed needs in two categories [ADR-17 D1; locked #6–7]; individuality from trait weight modifiers [ADR-10]; commitment from goals generated by five closed sources and filtered by ADR-01's five priority tiers [goal-system; locked #15]; persistence from a bounded memory pool that tilts future weights [ADR-16; memory-system]; and sociality from a closed six-action vocabulary [ADR-18 D1]. Within the winning tier, selection is weighted and seeded-stochastic — reproducible from state plus seed, but never a script [decision-loop §4; locked #24].

Everything a colonist does is: observable through seven ambient behavioral states [ADR-05; locked #29], explainable through decomposable causes at the inspector [locked #25, #27; ADR-14], deterministic under replay [Principle 7], and immune to direct command — by the player, forever, structurally [locked #3; ADR-07].

This document specifies that behavior end to end: the lifecycle (§2), the pipeline stage by stage (§3), the invariants (§4), the explainability and determinism contracts (§5–6), interruption rules (§7), social integration (§8), expected failures and acceptable emergence (§9), visibility per audience (§10), and the exact boundary between specification and engineering (§11).

## 2. Colonist Behavioral Lifecycle

The lifecycle has two alternating conditions and one closed transition set [decision-loop §2]:

**Executing.** The colonist performs their committed task's actions. Their observable behavior is one of the seven ambient states with its movement/posture textures [ADR-05; locked #29]. Need levels decay against the clock [ADR-02; ADR-17 D2]; stress accumulates or dissipates from its six sources and four reliefs [decision-loop §7]; affinity drifts through ambient channels [ADR-12]; the world evolves. The colonist does **not** re-evaluate their commitment while executing — commitment stickiness is structural, not incidental [decision-loop §2; locked #23].

**Deciding.** At a decision point, the colonist takes a fixed snapshot [decision-loop §1b], generates candidates from the five sources [goal-system], filters by tier [ADR-01], selects by composed weight [decision-loop §4, §6], resolves the adopted goal to a task [decision-loop §5], commits (recording motivation at that moment [goal-system; locked #16]), and returns to executing.

**The transition set is closed:** the loop re-enters deciding only on one of the six re-decision triggers — completion, higher-priority interruption, blockage, need threshold crossing, shift boundary condition, or resolution of a suspending condition [decision-loop §2; locked #23] — with ADR-18's one scoping refinement: a social offer reaches a colonist as an interruption-class trigger only when they are in an interruptible state [ADR-18 D5]. No clock value ever triggers a decision [ADR-02; locked #23]. No player input ever reaches the loop [locked #3; decision-loop B6].

**Lifecycle of the goal itself** runs through the six frozen phases: Candidate → Adopted → Active → (Interrupted | Blocked) → Completed/Abandoned, where interruption suspends and re-decides (never hard-resumes, never discards) and Blocked is an infrastructure signal distinct from Abandoned's character signal [goal-system; locked #17].

**Consequence flow closes the loop:** executed behavior changes the world and the colonist — outcomes move affinity [ADR-12], accumulate or relieve stress [decision-loop §7], restore or fail to restore needs [ADR-17 D2], and form memories when they meet ADR-16's significance criteria — and those changes are the inputs to every future decision [freeze §3: "today's conditions become tomorrow's character"].

## 3. Behavioral Pipeline

The sixteen stages below are the behavioral specification of the frozen cycle — the decision loop's seven conceptual stages (Perceive → Generate → Filter → Select → Resolve → Commit → Execute) plus the consequence phase the frozen end-to-end flow already defines [freeze §3]. **This granularity is presentation for engineering reference, not a new decision architecture; the frozen commitment that "stages are not an algorithm" applies to these sixteen exactly as it applies to the seven** [decision-loop B3]. Engineering may structure computation freely under the constraints; it may not change what each stage is specified to mean.

### 3.1 World Observation
The world evolves independently of any colonist: the continuous clock advances all rates uniformly [ADR-02]; module health follows the maintenance model [ADR-09]; conditions arise and resolve; other colonists act. Nothing in the world "notifies" a colonist — world change reaches a colonist only through their next snapshot, or by matching one of the six re-decision triggers [decision-loop §1b, §2]. Events in the world are condition-triggered, never clock-triggered [ADR-02].

### 3.2 Perception
At a decision point, the colonist receives one coherent, fixed snapshot [locked #4; architecture-philosophy §3]. Contents, closed [decision-loop §1b]: time references (elapsed durations, never triggering clock values); the colonist's cascade-resolved effective policy [ADR-11]; relevant module/resource conditions (health states as facts; **never crisis stage labels** [locked #22]); active survival conditions [ADR-01 P1]; and nearby colonists' locations and Tier-1 behavioral states only — a colonist knows about another colonist exactly what the player can see [locked #21]. Perception is spatially bounded to the colonist's module and immediately connected spaces (operational bounds deferred, DQ-D2) [decision-loop §1b]. The snapshot does not change during the decision [Principle 7].

### 3.3 Internal State Evaluation
The colonist's own model is directly available, not snapshot-mediated [decision-loop §1a]: stable identity (name, base traits, skills), long-term state (memory pool, relationship records, trait modifiers), short-term state (need levels, stress, goal stack, current behavioral state) [colonist-agent-model; locked #1]. No colonist ever evaluates another colonist's internals — the source of truth for another colonist's state is never accessible [colonist-agent-model; decision-loop §1b].

### 3.4 Need Assessment
Each of the five needs — Hunger, Rest, Safety, Social, Purpose; a closed taxonomy [ADR-17 D1; locked #6] — is one bounded continuous level that has been decaying monotonically against the clock at trait-modified rates [ADR-17 D2, D7]. Assessment reads each level against its thresholds [ADR-17 D3]: past the low threshold, the need contributes deferred urgency (tier 4); past the critical threshold — **which only Hunger and Rest possess** [ADR-17 D3–D4; locked #7] — override urgency (tier 2). Urgency grows monotonically with threshold depth and is computed per need, independently [ADR-17 D5]. Sustained Rest deprivation amplifies the urgency of other needs — **but only urgency that already exists; the amplifier never creates urgency for a satisfied need** ("a well-fed, exhausted colonist is exhausted, not hungry") [ADR-17 D6, review-revised].

### 3.5 Stress Evaluation
Stress is the colonist's emergent load — not a need, with no satisfaction action [needs-system P7; locked #8]. Its level reflects accumulation from the six traceable sources (sustained unmet psychological needs, sustained biological strain, hostile/Fractured proximity, crisis exposure, overwork, memory amplification) net of the four reliefs (adequate rest, satisfied needs, Bonded proximity, sustained stable conditions), with Stress Response traits scaling both directions [decision-loop §7; locked #27]. Evaluation feeds four downstream effects [decision-loop §7]: a weight family (§3.9); the Stressed ambient state past the behavioral threshold [ADR-05]; effective task-capacity suppression [ADR-09's solvability constraint]; and memory-formation significance [ADR-16].

### 3.6 Goal Generation
Candidates arise from exactly five closed sources, mapped one-to-one onto ADR-01's tiers [goal-system; locked #15]: (1) active survival conditions → tier 1; (2) critical biological need thresholds → tier 2; (3) the shift assignment under effective policy → tier 3; (4) low need thresholds → tier 4; (5) trait/relationship/memory-weighted voluntary possibilities — including ADR-18's sought social actions — → tier 5 [ADR-18 D3]. All sources produce candidates; nothing commands [freeze §3]. No mechanism may inject a goal outside these sources [locked #15].

### 3.7 Priority Resolution
ADR-01's order, applied verbatim [decision-loop §3]: candidates group by tier; the highest tier containing at least one *actionable* candidate wins; all lower tiers are eliminated from this decision. Actionable means at least one eligible, available task serves the candidate [decision-loop §3]. A candidate with no executable task is blocked, not eliminated — it persists in the stack and is re-checked at every re-decision [decision-loop §3]. **Fall-through:** if every candidate in the winning tier is blocked, selection falls to the next actionable tier; if no tier is actionable, the colonist enters the Blocked ambient state — motionless, not resting, not on task [ADR-05; decision-loop §3]. Tier 1 is adopted unconditionally: trait-immune, weight-immune, no selection of any kind [ADR-01; personality-traits B6; locked #12].

### 3.8 Candidate Selection
Within the winning tier (tiers 2–5; one mechanism for all [decision-loop §4]), when multiple candidates are actionable, selection samples among them in proportion to their composed weights using the save-seeded PRNG [locked #24; Principle 7]. When one weight dominates, the outcome is effectively deterministic; when weights are close, either outcome is a truthful "close call" [decision-loop §4]. Exact ties break by a fixed, stable, deterministic ordering (content is engineering's, DQ-D3; determinism is not negotiable) [decision-loop §4].

### 3.9 Weight Composition
Every candidate's weight is composed from one base and four modifier families [decision-loop §6; locked #25]. **Base:** urgency from current state — need threshold depth (Rest-amplified per §3.4), active assignment presence, free-period openness [decision-loop §6; ADR-17 D5]. **Families:** Traits (standing leans, keyed on candidate class/context, with active trait modifiers applied) [ADR-10; personality-traits]; Relationships (social gravity within ADR-12's influence zones, keyed on the specific colonists involved or nearby) [ADR-12]; Memory (active memories whose person / resource-kind / situation-kind matches the candidate, proportional to fading influence weight) [ADR-16; decision-loop §8]; Stress (relief-serving candidates raised, demanding candidates suppressed, against trait-set thresholds) [decision-loop §7]. **The five constraints are behavioral law** [locked #25]: composition operates within tiers only; every modifier bounds and never vetoes; the composed weight is decomposable into its family contributions, always; the result is order-independent (or order-fixed and documented); co-held conflicting traits contribute as independent opposing tilts with no arbitration.

### 3.10 Goal Commitment
The selected candidate is adopted: it enters the goal stack as active; its **motivation is recorded at this moment** — the cause state at decision time, re-derived only at future re-decision points [goal-system; locked #16; ADR-14]. If the decision is significant per ADR-14's criteria, a decision-log entry is written carrying the decomposed causes [ADR-14; decision-loop §10]. The stack holds the active goal, suspended goals awaiting their resolution trigger, and queued adopted goals; queued goals whose motivation no longer holds are abandoned at re-decision [decision-loop §10].

### 3.11 Task Resolution
The adopted goal finds a concrete task from the five conceptual classes — Assignment, Satisfaction, Response, Social (populated by ADR-18's six actions), Transit-and-idle [decision-loop §5; locked #26; ADR-18 D1]. A task is **eligible** when colonist Skill ∩ policy Permission ∩ task Requirement intersect [locked #2, #26]; **available** when the world cooperates: functional module, present resource, unexhausted capacity, reachable location — read from the snapshot [decision-loop §5]. Among multiple serving tasks, the same weighted selection applies, shaped by proximity, condition quality, and destination relationship context (the avoidance and preference weights ADR-12 defines — how two colonists avoid each other without an avoidance script) [decision-loop §5].

### 3.12 Action Execution
The task executes as observable behavior: one of the seven ambient states — Working, Resting, Eating, Socializing, Stressed, Blocked, In Conflict — with movement/posture textures carrying trait, stress, and Purpose legibility [ADR-05; locked #29; ADR-05 confirmation record 2026-07-09]. During execution: social offers may arrive (answered per §8, only in interruptible states) [ADR-18 D5]; encounter conditions may conjoin (Confrontation, §8) [ADR-18 D3–D4]; the critical-state icon shows when applicable (the one Tier-1 icon exception) [ADR-05]. Execution continues until a re-decision trigger fires [decision-loop §2].

### 3.13 Observation of Results
Task outcomes are world facts, not private events: completed work changes module/resource state [ADR-09]; satisfaction restores need levels while conditions hold [ADR-17 D2]; failures change conditions. Completion is judged per goal category [decision-loop §10]: survival goals complete when the condition resolves; need goals at the satisfaction point (strictly above the low threshold — the structural hysteresis band) [ADR-17 D3]; assignment goals at the shift boundary condition [ADR-02]; voluntary goals are opportunity-bounded. **No goal completes on a clock value** [decision-loop §10]. Mid-execution failure (module down, resource gone) triggers re-decision with the goal persisting — blocked, not abandoned [decision-loop §5; locked #17].

### 3.14 Memory Formation
Formation is involuntary and trait-ungated [memory-system; locked #19]: an outcome that meets ADR-16's significance criteria (measurable affinity change, stress change, need-state change, or behavioral override) forms a memory with **impact fixed at formation** [ADR-16; locked #19], typed by the surface it will tilt — Relational, Deprivation, Crisis, or Condition; a closed list [memory-system; locked #18]. Each participant in a shared event forms (or does not form) their own memory independently [ADR-18 D9]. Routine texture does not form memories — memory is not the event log [locked #5]. The pool remains bounded; eviction removes lowest influence weight, never oldest [ADR-16].

### 3.15 Relationship Update
Affinity scores move only through ADR-12's accepted sources — shared task completion, forced proximity under stress, direct conflict, mutual support, avoidance atrophy, trait-compatibility drift — with ADR-18's actions instantiating (never extending) that table [ADR-12; ADR-18 D6]. Confrontation's effect is strictly negative; repair happens only through later positive interactions [ADR-18 D6, review-revised]. Relationship changes are **outputs of behavior, not inputs within the same decision**: today's interactions move scores that shape tomorrow's weights [decision-loop §9]. Discrete state transitions (Bonded, Hostile, Fractured...) follow from score bands and feed story events [ADR-12; ADR-08].

### 3.16 Re-decision Conditions
The closed six-trigger list, restated as the pipeline's only re-entry points [decision-loop §2; locked #23]: (1) task/goal completion; (2) appearance of a higher-priority condition — the active goal suspends; (3) blockage — preconditions fail; (4) a need threshold crossing generating a new candidate; (5) the shift boundary condition (duration elapsed AND not in a safety-critical task) [ADR-02]; (6) resolution of a suspending condition — re-decision with the suspended goal as default candidate. Plus the ADR-18 scoping: social offers act as trigger-2-class events only for colonists in interruptible states [ADR-18 D5]. Between triggers: execution continues, unconditionally [decision-loop §2].

## 4. Behavioral Principles

The invariants. Every colonist behavior, in every build, at every tuning, satisfies all of these:

1. **No direct commands — no mechanism, no exception, no crisis override.** The player reaches behavior only through conditions: policy, infrastructure, station design [locked #3; ADR-07; Pillar 2].
2. **Tier supremacy is absolute.** No weight, trait, relationship, memory, stress level, or social pull ever lifts a candidate across an ADR-01 tier boundary [locked #25; decision-loop §3]. Survival (tier 1) is adopted unconditionally and is trait-immune [locked #12].
3. **Psychological needs never override.** No value of Safety, Social, or Purpose — and no modifier upon them — produces a shift override; their escalation path is stress, only [ADR-17 D4; locked #7].
4. **Modifiers bound, never veto.** Nothing makes a behavior certain or impossible by rule; refusal is likely, not guaranteed; tendency is tilt, not script [locked #25; ADR-12; ADR-10].
5. **Tendencies are probabilistic; character is not a rule set.** Identical colonists in identical states may differ across saves (different seeds) but never within a replay (same seed) [decision-loop §4; Principle 7].
6. **Behavior is condition-triggered, never clock-triggered.** No decision, event, transition, or completion fires on a clock value [ADR-02; locked #23].
7. **Commitment sticks.** Between the six triggers a colonist does not re-litigate; oscillation is a defect, not a tuning outcome [decision-loop §2; goal-system Risk 3].
8. **Knowledge is local and symmetric with the player's.** A colonist knows their own model plus Tier-1 observables within perceptual range — nothing else, ever [locked #21; decision-loop §1b].
9. **Every closed list is closed.** Five needs [ADR-17 D1], five goal sources [locked #15], four trait categories [locked #11], four memory types [locked #18], five task classes [locked #26], six social actions [ADR-18 D1], seven ambient states [locked #29], six re-decision triggers [locked #23]. Extension is an ADR, never a feature.
10. **Everything observable, everything decomposable.** Every need expresses through a readable channel [needs-system P4]; every significant decision decomposes into its cause families [locked #25]; every stress movement traces to its sources [locked #27]; Purpose is never a hidden score [locked #9].
11. **Memory is not the event log; needs are not meters the player fills; stress is not a need** [locked #5, #8; needs-system P6–P7].
12. **Social participation is chosen on both sides.** Offers, weighted responses, legitimate declines; conflict emerges from conditions and is never adopted as a goal; compelled collaboration is not social [ADR-18 D3–D5, review-revised].

## 5. Explainability

What the explanation surfaces must always be able to answer, truthfully and legibly [Principle 6; ADR-14; decision-loop §11]:

- **"What are they doing?"** — the ambient state, at all times, Tier 1 [ADR-05].
- **"Why are they doing it?"** — the active goal, its tier, and its motivation as recorded at adoption ("adopted because...") [goal-system; locked #16], displayed beside live current state [decision-loop §11].
- **"Why this and not something else?"** — the decision's decomposed weight contributions by family: base urgency, traits (named as weighting, never as cause [colonist-agent-model B4]), relationships (the specific states), stress (with its source breakdown), and memory **when material** — named ("carries the Day 12 overload"), under the counterfactual-relevance test [locked #25, #28; decision-loop §8, §11].
- **"Why is their stress moving?"** — every stress movement decomposable into its sources ("inadequate rest, 3 shifts; shared module with Hostile colleague") [locked #27].
- **"Why did they refuse / decline / deviate?"** — refusals as weight outcomes with visible composition [decision-loop §7]; social declines explainable from the decliner's side [ADR-18 D10]; every deviation from the shift baseline traceable to the need, condition, or event behind it [needs-system; ADR-01].
- **"Why are they fighting?"** — every Confrontation traceable to its conjunction: relationship state, stress levels, shared space [ADR-18 D10].
- **"What happened, historically?"** — the event log (world-owned, permanent) and the decision log's significant entries with their Why lines spanning all five cause families, memory included under materiality [ADR-14; ADR-16 clarification record 2026-07-09].
- **What is never shown:** the memory pool as a browsable list; other colonists' internals; weight numbers as optimizable arithmetic — the explanation vocabulary is causes and leanings [decision-loop §11; locked #20].

The structural guarantee behind all of it: decomposability is a property of the weight composition itself, not a UI afterthought [locked #25; decision-loop §11]. An implementation that cannot produce these answers at every significant decision is out of specification, whatever else it does well [decision-loop Risk 2 mitigation].

## 6. Determinism

The replay contract [Principle 7; architecture-philosophy]:

- **State + seed → identical behavior.** Given identical serialized simulation state and the same save seed, every colonist makes the identical decision at every decision point, indefinitely [decision-loop §4; locked #24].
- **The seeded PRNG is the only chance source.** All stochastic selection (goal selection, task selection, encounter triggering [ADR-18 D4]) draws from the save-seeded PRNG; unseeded randomness is prohibited anywhere in behavior [decision-loop §4; Principle 7].
- **Need dynamics are chance-free.** Decay, thresholds, restoration, and stress movement are pure state evolution; nothing stochastic touches a need or stress level [ADR-17 D2].
- **Snapshots are fixed.** No mid-decision reads, no live cross-agent references — decision inputs cannot change during the decision [locked #4].
- **Composition is order-independent or order-fixed.** The composed weight must not vary with modifier application order; if the realization is order-sensitive, the order is fixed and documented [locked #25].
- **Ties break deterministically.** A fixed, stable ordering criterion (DQ-D3) — its content is engineering's choice; its stability across runs is not [decision-loop §4].
- **All behavioral state is serializable.** Need levels, stress, goal stack with motivations, memory pool with influence weights, relationship records, trait modifiers, and the PRNG state itself save and load exactly [architecture-philosophy; colonist-agent-model].
- **Replay guarantee, stated for engineering:** a saved game replayed from the same state and seed reproduces every decision, every behavior, every story — bit-for-bit at the behavioral level. Divergence under replay is a defect of the highest class, because it falsifies the explanation surfaces retroactively [Principle 7; Principle 6].

## 7. Interruptions

**Behavior may be interrupted when — and only when — a re-decision trigger fires** [decision-loop §2; locked #23]. The interruption semantics:

**May interrupt:**
- **A higher-priority condition** (trigger 2): a survival condition interrupts anything [ADR-01 P1]; a critical biological need interrupts tiers 3–5 [ADR-01 P2]. The interrupted goal suspends — it is neither discarded nor hard-resumed; when the interrupting condition resolves (trigger 6), re-decision runs with the suspended goal as default candidate [goal-system; locked #17].
- **Blockage and task failure** (trigger 3, and §3.13's mid-execution failures): the world withdrawing a task's preconditions forces re-decision; the goal persists as blocked [decision-loop §5].
- **A social offer — narrowly:** only for colonists in interruptible states, as an interruption-class event; the response is the partner's own weighted decision [ADR-18 D5].

**May not interrupt:**
- **Nothing interrupts a tier-1 survival response except its own completion or a superseding survival condition** [ADR-01].
- **The shift boundary does not interrupt a safety-critical task**: a colonist mid-emergency-repair at boundary completes the repair first, then transitions [ADR-02].
- **No social offer interrupts a higher-tier commitment**: a Working colonist mid-assignment is not re-decided by a Conversation offer — the offer fails as unavailable and the initiator re-resolves [ADR-18 D5].
- **The player interrupts nothing, ever** — no pause-and-redirect, no emergency channel; the player changes conditions (ADR-07 Categories 1–3) and colonists respond at their own decision points [locked #3; ADR-07].
- **The clock interrupts nothing** [ADR-02].

Between triggers, execution is unconditional (Principle 7 of §4: commitment sticks). Interruption *frequency* is therefore a function of world volatility, not of any per-tick evaluation — a stable colony produces long uninterrupted commitments, and that is the design [decision-loop §2].

## 8. Social Behavior

Social behavior is not a parallel system — it is the same pipeline with ADR-18's vocabulary flowing through it [ADR-18 throughout]:

- **Generation:** sought actions (Conversation, Shared Downtime, Comfort, Assist) arise as tier-4 candidates from the Social need's low threshold and tier-5 candidates from trait/relationship/memory-weighted voluntary possibility [ADR-18 D3; goal-system source 4–5].
- **Initiation reads only what is knowable:** the initiator's own records plus partners' Tier-1 observable states — Comfort targets the visible Stressed state; Assist targets observable Working/Blocked colonists on work the initiator is eligible for (the eligibility intersection applies; Assist never bypasses it) [ADR-18 D4].
- **Assist is voluntary or it is not social:** policy-assigned collaboration is tier-3 assignment work, produces relational effect only through ambient drift channels, and credits no Social need [ADR-18 D1/D3, review-revised].
- **Participation is two-sided:** offers, not commands; the partner's acceptance is their own weighted composition (relationship, stress, traits, memories); decline is legitimate, observable at Tier 1 as approach-and-part texture, and explainable at Tier 3 [ADR-18 D5, D10].
- **Confrontation is never chosen:** it triggers as an encounter when a Hostile/Fractured pair shares space past combined stress thresholds — probabilistic via the seeded PRNG, never guaranteed — and expresses as In Conflict [ADR-18 D3–D4]. Its affinity effect is strictly negative; repair flows only through later positive interactions [ADR-18 D6, review-revised].
- **Shared Meal is the one two-need action:** a Hunger-satisfaction task whose social crediting activates in non-hostile company — Hunger and Social, never Purpose [ADR-18 D3/D7, review-revised; ADR-17 D6].
- **Crediting requires participation:** opportunity is the availability condition (the player's lever); participation is the satisfaction event (the colonist's behavior); proximity alone credits nothing; Confrontation credits nothing; social actions never credit Purpose [ADR-18 D7; ADR-17 D9].
- **Consequences route through existing channels only:** ADR-12's affinity sources, the decision loop's stress sources/reliefs, ADR-16's memory formation — zero social-specific mechanics [ADR-18 D6, D8, D9].
- **Groups are emergent:** pairwise interactions overlapping in shared space; no group entity exists [ADR-18 D5].
- **Ambient expression:** Socializing and In Conflict carry the primary signals; Eating and Working carry the overlay/Assist textures; six actions, four states, no eighth state [ADR-18 D10; locked #29].

## 9. Failure Modes

The specification distinguishes **acceptable emergent behavior** (the simulation being truthful) from **behavioral defects** (violations of this specification). Engineering and playtesting must not "fix" the first category or tolerate the second.

**Acceptable — these are the design working:**
- **A colonist walks into bad news** — pursuing a task toward a module they cannot know has failed. Spatial honesty; legible; story-productive [decision-loop Risk 5, accepted as partly intended].
- **A distressed colonist is ignored** — nobody's weights favored Comfort (traits, relationships, own load). The colony that doesn't care for someone is a true story the architecture must keep possible [ADR-18 Risks].
- **A close call goes the "other" way** — near-even weights resolving by seeded chance. That is a person for whom it genuinely was a close call [decision-loop §4].
- **A colonist declines a friend under stress** — life, not betrayal; low-magnitude friction at most [ADR-18 D5].
- **A Blocked colonist stands motionless** — things to do and a world that won't allow any of them; exactly the signal the player needs [decision-loop §3; ADR-05].
- **The indispensable engineer nobody talks to; the well-liked colonist with no purpose** — both configurations must remain producible [needs-system; ADR-17 D6].
- **Colonists deviate from a bad policy** — gradually, visibly, traceably. The Need System landing the player's conditions on people [needs-system Purpose].
- **Veterans behave consistently (not identically) after shared history** — memory convergence within the frozen individuality guarantees [memory-system; freeze Risk: convergence monoculture, prototype watch].

**Defects — out of specification, always:**
- **Oscillation:** commitment flip-flopping between triggers, or satisfy/re-trigger cycling at a threshold (the hysteresis band exists to prevent this) [decision-loop §2; ADR-17 D3].
- **Untraceable behavior:** any significant decision, stress movement, or need change the inspector cannot decompose [locked #25, #27; needs-system Need Interaction exclusion].
- **Cross-tier violations:** any modifier lifting a candidate over a tier boundary; any psychological need overriding; any trait affecting a tier-1 response [§4.2–4.4].
- **Scripted reads:** any behavior that is certain — the guaranteed refusal, the automatic confrontation, the trait that always fires. Certainty where the architecture specifies weight is a script [locked #25].
- **Replay divergence** [§6].
- **Baseline collapse:** critical overrides so frequent the shift skeleton is unlearnable — a calibration defect against the binding target (overrides are exceptional events, not daily texture) [ADR-01; needs-system Risk 3; ADR-17 D10].
- **Dead colony / flooded colony:** story-event frequency outside ADR-08's floor-to-fatigue band; conflict as daily noise [ADR-08; ADR-18 deferred targets].
- **Behavior the player reads as random rather than as character** — ADR-10's own revisit trigger, applying to trait expression and to flat weight spreads alike [ADR-10; decision-loop Risk 1].

## 10. Inspector Visibility

Three audiences, three contracts:

**Player — ambient (Tier 1) and hover (Tier 2)** [ADR-05]:
- Always visible: the seven behavioral states with their textures; module health states [ADR-13]; the critical-state icon exception (critical needs; Fractured relationships) [ADR-05; ADR-12].
- On hover: categorical need summary (critical/low/normal), social state, current task, stress indicator [ADR-05].
- Never ambient: need numbers, memory, weights, internals. The scan loop reads behavior, not telemetry [ADR-05; needs-system P4].

**Player — inspector (Tier 3)** [ADR-05; ADR-14; decision-loop §11]:
- All need levels with history (a chronically low psychological need must be visually undeniable [needs-system Risk 1]); full relationship list with states and bounded interaction history [ADR-12]; current goal, tier, and recorded motivation; discovered traits with modifiers as sub-entries, undiscovered as "Unknown" [ADR-10]; decision detail with decomposed cause families; stress source breakdown; material memories, named [locked #20, #28]; the decision log and event log [ADR-14].
- Never in the inspector: the memory pool as a list; other colonists' internals through this colonist's view; optimizable weight arithmetic — causes and leanings only [decision-loop §11].

**Debug tools (engineering-facing, development builds)** — *a specification-level clarification, not new architecture:* every player-facing visibility constraint above binds **player surfaces**; the frozen architecture's invisibility commitments (ADR-16 ambient invisibility, no-internals perception, no weight numbers) are commitments about the *game*, not about engineering's ability to verify it. Debug tooling may expose the full truth — complete need/stress values, the entire memory pool with influence weights, full weight compositions, snapshot contents, PRNG state, re-decision trigger history — because §5–§6's contracts (decomposability, determinism, replay) are unverifiable without it [locked #25's decomposability presumes retention; Principle 7's reproducibility presumes state access]. Two binding constraints: debug surfaces must never ship in the player build's UI, and debug tooling must be read-only toward behavior — a debug tool that *changes* colonist state in a live game is a command channel and violates locked #3's spirit. The tooling's design is engineering scope (§11).

## 12. Risks

Remaining *behavioral* risks only (architecture risks stand as recorded in freeze §7; ADR-17/18 risks stand in their ADRs):

- **The pipeline reads as a checklist.** Sixteen named stages invite engineering to implement sixteen sequential steps and then treat the structure as the behavior. *Mitigation: decision-loop B3 restated at §3's head; the stages define meaning, not computation.*
- **The acceptable/defect boundary (§9) erodes in playtesting.** Pressure to "fix" the ignored distressed colonist or the unlucky close call would script away the simulation's truthfulness. *Mitigation: §9 is explicit precisely so triage conversations have a reference; changes to the boundary go through design review, not bug triage.*
- **Debug tooling leaks into player experience** — a dev overlay shipping half-disabled, or debug numbers screenshotted into community optimization guides shaping play against the causes-and-leanings intent. *Mitigation: §10's binding constraint (never in player build UI); the read-only rule.*
- **Cumulative texture load** (carried forward, now with ADR-18's six actions added to traits, Purpose erosion, and memory drift all expressing through the same seven states at overview zoom): the freeze's most honest open exposure, still unowned until the prototype. *Mitigation: unchanged — first-order prototype evaluation target [freeze §7].*
- **Specification drift.** This document summarizes ten authoritative sources; future revisions of any source could silently desynchronize it. *Mitigation: the traceability bracketing — every statement names its source, so a source change identifies its dependents mechanically; this document must be revised whenever an upstream document is.*
